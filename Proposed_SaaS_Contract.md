## 2. FEES AND RENEWAL

**2.1 Auto-Renewal.** Subscriptions automatically renew for successive 12-month terms ("Subscription Term"). Customer must provide written cancellation notice no fewer than **ninety (90) days** before the end of the current Subscription Term. Provider shall deliver written notice of any upcoming renewal at least ninety (90) days prior to the renewal date, including confirmation of any fee changes for the renewal term. If Provider fails to provide such notice, Customer may cancel the renewal within thirty (30) days of receiving actual notice of the renewal without penalty and shall receive a pro-rata refund of any fees paid for the period following cancellation.

**2.2 Fee Increases.** Fees for any renewal term shall not increase by more than the lesser of: (i) five percent (5%) of the fees for the prior Subscription Term; or (ii) the percentage increase in the U.S. Consumer Price Index for All Urban Consumers (CPI-U) for the prior twelve (12) months. Any proposed fee increase must be communicated to Customer in writing at least ninety (90) days prior to renewal. Any purported increase in excess of the foregoing cap shall be void and of no effect.

## 3. SERVICE LEVELS

**3.1 Uptime Commitment.** Provider shall maintain a monthly uptime availability of at least **99.9%** ("Uptime SLA"), measured on a calendar-month basis and excluding scheduled maintenance windows communicated to Customer at least five (5) business days in advance. "Uptime" means the percentage of total minutes in a calendar month during which the Services are available and operational, excluding scheduled maintenance.

**3.2 Service Credits.** In the event Provider fails to meet the Uptime SLA in any calendar month, Customer shall be eligible for service credits as follows:

- (i) Uptime below 99.9% but at or above 99.0%: credit equal to **10%** of monthly fees;
- (ii) Uptime below 99.0% but at or above 95.0%: credit equal to **25%** of monthly fees;
- (iii) Uptime below 95.0%: credit equal to **50%** of monthly fees.

Service credits shall be applied to the following month's invoice. Service credits shall not be Customer's sole or exclusive remedy for Provider's failure to meet the Uptime SLA. Customer may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if Provider fails to meet the Uptime SLA for three (3) or more months in any rolling twelve (12)-month period, and shall receive a pro-rata refund of all prepaid, unused fees.

**3.3 Maintenance.** Provider shall provide Customer with at least forty-eight (48) hours' advance written notice of any scheduled maintenance. Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to schedule maintenance during off-peak hours (weekdays between 10:00 PM and 6:00 AM Eastern Time, or weekends). Emergency maintenance required to address active security incidents or system failures shall be excluded from the Uptime SLA calculation, provided Provider notifies Customer as soon as practicable.

**3.4 Security Certifications.** Provider shall maintain at minimum a SOC 2 Type II certification throughout the Subscription Term. Provider shall provide Customer with a copy of its then-current SOC 2 Type II report upon written request. Provider shall promptly notify Customer in the event its SOC 2 Type II certification lapses or is materially qualified.

- (i) use Customer Data to train, fine-tune, or otherwise improve machine learning or artificial intelligence models;
- (ii) aggregate, de-identify, or anonymize Customer Data for use in benchmarking, product development, or any commercial purpose;
- (iii) sell, license, sublicense, or transfer Customer Data to any third party, except as strictly necessary to provide the Services and subject to the data processing obligations in Section 4.3 and the Data Processing Agreement; or
- (iv) use Customer Data to develop, improve, or market any product or service.

**4.2 Limited License Grant.** Customer grants Provider a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable, non-sublicensable license to access and process Customer Data solely to the extent necessary to provide the Services during the Subscription Term. This license terminates immediately upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason.

The DPA shall govern Provider's processing of any personal data included within Customer Data and shall address, at minimum: lawful basis for processing, sub-processor disclosure and notification obligations, data subject rights procedures, international transfer mechanisms (Standard Contractual Clauses or equivalent), and Provider's obligations as a data processor. In the event of any conflict between this Agreement and the DPA with respect to the processing of personal data, the DPA shall control.

- **(I) EITHER PARTY'S INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS UNDER SECTION 6;**
- **(II) EITHER PARTY'S BREACH OF ITS CONFIDENTIALITY OBLIGATIONS;**
- **(III) PROVIDER'S BREACH OF ITS DATA SECURITY OBLIGATIONS OR UNAUTHORIZED USE OF CUSTOMER DATA UNDER SECTION 4;**
- **(IV) DAMAGES ARISING FROM A PARTY'S GROSS NEGLIGENCE, FRAUD, OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT; OR**
- **(V) CUSTOMER'S PAYMENT OBLIGATIONS FOR FEES DULY OWED UNDER THIS AGREEMENT.**

**5.2 Mutual Exclusion of Consequential Damages.** IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, PUNITIVE, OR EXEMPLARY DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOSS OF PROFITS, DATA, BUSINESS, OR GOODWILL, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

**NOTWITHSTANDING THE FOREGOING, THE EXCLUSION IN THIS SECTION 5.2 SHALL NOT APPLY TO:**

- **(I) DAMAGES ARISING FROM A PARTY'S BREACH OF ITS CONFIDENTIALITY OBLIGATIONS;**

**5.3 Applicability.** The foregoing limitations apply regardless of the theory of liability — contract, tort, strict liability, or otherwise — and shall apply even if any limited remedy fails of its essential purpose. ## 9. SECURITY INCIDENT NOTIFICATION

**9.1 Notice Obligation.** Provider shall notify Customer of any confirmed or reasonably suspected Security Incident affecting Customer Data within **seventy-two (72) hours** of Provider's discovery of such incident. Notice shall be provided to Customer's designated security contact or, in the absence of such designation, to Customer's primary legal or IT contact.

**9.2 Notice Content.** Such notice shall include, to the extent then known and without undue delay:

- (i) a description of the nature of the Security Incident, including the categories of Customer Data involved;
- (ii) the categories and approximate number of individuals and data records affected;
- (iii) the name and contact information of Provider's data protection officer or designated security contact;
- (iv) the likely consequences of the Security Incident; and
- (v) the measures taken or proposed to be taken by Provider to address the Security Incident and to mitigate its possible adverse effects.

**9.3 Definition.** "Security Incident" means any unauthorized access to, acquisition of, disclosure of, alteration of, or destruction of Customer Data, or any breach of Provider's physical, technical, administrative, or organizational safeguards that compromises the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of Customer Data.

**9.4 Cooperation.** Provider shall cooperate fully with Customer and, where required by applicable law, with relevant supervisory authorities in connection with any Security Incident, including by providing Customer with all information reasonably necessary for Customer to meet its own legal notification and reporting obligations.

## 10. DATA RETURN AND DELETION

**10.1 Data Export.** Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement for any reason, Customer shall have sixty (60) days to export or retrieve all Customer Data from the Services in a machine-readable, industry-standard format (the "Data Return Period"). Provider shall make the Services available to Customer in read-only mode for this purpose and shall cooperate with and provide reasonable assistance to Customer in connection with such export at no additional charge.

**10.2 Deletion.** Following the Data Return Period, Provider shall securely delete all Customer Data from its systems, including all production databases, backup systems, and disaster recovery systems, within thirty (30) days. Provider shall provide Customer with written certification of such deletion upon Customer's request.

**10.3 Survival.** Provider's obligations under this Section 10 shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason and shall not be affected by any other provision of this Agreement.

## 11. TERMINATION

**11.1 Termination for Convenience.** Customer may terminate this Agreement for any reason or no reason upon sixty (60) days' prior written notice to Provider. In the event of Customer's termination for convenience, Provider shall refund to Customer any prepaid, unused fees on a pro-rata basis for the remainder of the current Subscription Term within thirty (30) days of the effective date of termination.

**11.2 Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other party:

- (i) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days of written notice specifying the nature of the breach in reasonable detail; or
- (ii) becomes insolvent, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, or becomes subject to bankruptcy, receivership, or similar proceedings that are not dismissed within sixty (60) days.

**11.3 Termination by Customer for SLA Failure.** Customer may terminate this Agreement in accordance with Section 3.2 for Provider's repeated failure to meet the Uptime SLA.

**11.4 Effect of Termination.** Upon any termination or expiration of this Agreement: (i) all rights and licenses granted to Customer hereunder shall immediately terminate; (ii) Customer shall pay any outstanding fees due through the effective date of termination; (iii) Sections 4.1, 5, 6, 7, 8, 10, 11.4, and 12 shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement; and (iv) Provider shall comply with its data return and deletion obligations under Section 10.
